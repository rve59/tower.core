## 6. Financial Data Prognosis

### **6.1 Market Scope & Reporting Entities**
The TOWER addressable market is defined by the rigid regulatory mandates of the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC). Reporting is not optional; it is a "License to Operate" requirement for all jurisdictional energy entities.

#### **Primary Industry Segments**
1.  **Electric (Major & Non-Major)**: Vertically integrated utilities, transmission providers, and balancing authorities.
2.  **Natural Gas**: Interstate pipeline companies and storage providers.
3.  **Oil Pipelines**: Interstate oil and petroleum product pipeline carriers.
4.  **Service Companies**: Centralized service subsidiaries of large holding companies.

#### **Entity Inventory (Total Addressable Market)**
| Reporting Form | Entity Type | Est. Annual Filer Count | Filing Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Form 1 / 1-F** | Electric (Major/Non-Major) | ~240 | Annual + Quarterly (3-Q) |
| **Form 2 / 2-A** | Natural Gas | ~165 | Annual + Quarterly (3-Q) |
| **Form 6** | Oil Pipelines | ~180 | Annual + Quarterly (6-Q) |
| **Form 60** | Service Companies | ~50 | Annual |
| **Form 714** | Balancing Authorities | ~180 | Annual |
| **EQR** | Market Participants | **~3,670** | **Quarterly (High Volume)** |

**TOTAL HIGH-INTENSITY FILERS**: **~635+** (The "Enterprise" target).  
**TOTAL HIGH-VOLUME RESPONDENTS**: **~3,670+** (The "Agentic/Volume" target).

> [!NOTE]
> **Market Entity Discrepancies Explained**:
> *   **Holding Company Overlap (815 vs 635+)**: The sum of individual form filers (~815) exceeds the target enterprise count (~635+) because large utility holding companies (e.g., Duke Energy) must file multiple form types across their subsidiaries (e.g., Form 1 for electric, Form 2 for gas).
> *   **EQR vs. Infrastructure Forms (3,670 vs 635+)**: Annual Forms (1, 2, 6) are exhaustive financial reports required only of asset-heavy "Major" infrastructure owners. The **EQR (Electric Quarterly Report)** is required from *any* entity that sells wholesale power. The ~3,670 EQR count includes thousands of independent solar/wind farms, battery operators, and pure-financial trading desks (power marketers) that own no physical infrastructure but participate in the wholesale markets.
